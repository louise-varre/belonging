The nervous system

At Eldvarm we want to create moments of being, in a world busy doing. The body is made for both, to act and to rest, and to move between them. What returned us to rest were the things we did each night around the fire: we gathered, ate together, sang, sat close and told stories as the light went down. The fire was where we did them, but they were never bound to it, and they are still here, still ours, available to us at any time.

The two systems

The sympathetic nervous system activates when the body needs to act, in physical effort, excitement, challenge, or perceived threat. Heart rate and blood pressure rise, focus and sensory acuity sharpen, and blood redirects to the muscles, while digestion, immune function and repair are suppressed for the duration. It is built for short bursts, minutes of intense activation followed by full recovery, and it is chronic activation without that recovery that drives inflammation, immune suppression and burnout. It is the essential drive to act, move and respond, without which we would lack the energy to find food, protect ourselves, or push forward.

The parasympathetic nervous system activates when the body registers safety. Heart rate slows, blood pressure drops, digestion and nutrient absorption come online, and tissue repair and immune function strengthen. Its main pathway is the vagus nerve, the longest cranial nerve in the body, running from the brainstem to the abdomen. This is also the state we reach together rather than alone: humans are co-regulators, steadying one another into safety through voice, touch, scent, breath and simple closeness. It is the ground of rest, repair and recovery, without which the body cannot rebuild and its long-term function breaks down.
