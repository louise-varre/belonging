Belonging

For as long as humans have existed, our survival depended on belonging to the group. Social exclusion was a near-certain death, which is why our brain shaped itself to recognise belonging as safety. Every evening, our ancestors did activities together that strengthened the social connections of the group and produced the feeling of safety the body needs to rest, digest, and regenerate. Although our world looks very different today, our brain is essentially the same. We still need a strong sense of belonging to thrive.

We are co-regulators, and throughout the exhibition we look at the different factors that create the sense of belonging we need: the warmth and light of the fire that drew us together, and the simple things we have done around it for over 400,000 years. We take each one in turn, with the science of how it still calms our nervous system today.

Before you leave, we would love to know, where do you belong?
